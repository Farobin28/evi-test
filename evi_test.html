However, he decided to drop out because he was deeply interested in working with computers. He soon got a job at Microsoft, where he spent 13 years helping to create the early Windows operating systems.

2. Despite his success and high salary at Microsoft, Gabe had a different passion: video games. In 1996, he took a massive risk, left his safe job, and founded his own company called Valve. 3. Valve didn't stop at making just one successful game. However, Gabe noticed a significant problem in the market. It completely revolutionized the market. Now, anyone who makes a high-quality game can publish it online and reach millions of players globally.

Maintaining this stable infrastructure is a huge technical achievement.

Task 2: Reading (Multiple Choice)

The Appeal of Survival Games and Networks

Over the last decade, a specific genre has taken the gaming world by storm: the multiplayer survival game. Unlike traditional games where players are given clear missions and powerful weapons from the start, survival games drop players into a harsh environment with absolutely nothing. The primary goal is simple yet incredibly challenging: stay alive, find resources, and build a shelter.

However, the virtual environment is not the only thing players have to worry about. The true tension comes from interacting with other human players sharing the same server. This requires a perfectly stable internet connection. Steam makes it easy to connect to these servers, but behind the scenes, the technology is massive.

Millions of players log in daily. If a server crashes or the network goes down, the entire player experience is ruined. This is why companies hire dedicated network engineers, systems administrators, and IT specialists. Their job is to monitor the infrastructure, configure network protocols, and troubleshoot any software or hardware issues immediately.

For young professionals interested in technology, this sector offers exciting career opportunities. It takes a lot of patience to write code, design levels with programs like Unity, or maintain server racks. But the result—a stable, engaging global system—is a massive technical achievement that keeps the modern entertainment industry running.
